UGGANI

description

Uggani is a traditional Andhra dish made from puffed rice (murmura) mixed with onions, green chilies, turmeric, and spices. This light, savory dish is often served with mirchi bajji (spicy green chili fritters). It is a popular breakfast or snack option, known for its simplicity and flavorful kick.

Ingredients

  • Puffed Rice (Murmura): 4 cups
  • Oil: 2-3 tablespoons
  • Cumin Seeds: 1 teaspoon
  • Dry Red Chillies: 2-3, broken into pieces
  • Curry Leaves: 8-10 leaves
  • Green Chillies: 2-3, finely chopped
  • Onion: 1 large, finely chopped
  • Turmeric Powder: 1/2 teaspoon
  • Salt: To taste
  • Lemon Juice: 1-2 tablespoons
  • Coriander Leaves: 2 tablespoons, chopped (for garnish)
  • Sev or Peanuts (optional): For added crunch and flavor

steps

  1. Finely chop onions, green chilies, and coriander leaves.
  2. Break dry red chilies into pieces.
  3. Heat 2-3 tablespoons of oil in a large pan or kadhai.
  4. Add 1 teaspoon of mustard seeds and 1 teaspoon of cumin seeds to the hot oil.
  5. Once the seeds start to splutter, add 2-3 broken dry red chilies and 8-10 curry leaves. Sauté for a few seconds.
  6. Add finely chopped green chilies and 1 large finely chopped onion to the pan.
  7. Sauté until the onions turn translucent.
  8. Sprinkle 1/2 teaspoon of turmeric powder and salt to taste. Mix well.
  9. Add 4 cups of puffed rice (murmura) to the pan. Mix gently to coat the puffed rice evenly with the onion-spice mixture
  10. Cook for 2-3 minutes, stirring continuously to ensure the puffed rice absorbs the flavors and gets heated through.
  11. Turn off the heat and squeeze 1-2 tablespoons of lemon juice over the Uggani.
  12. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves.
  13. Optionally, you can add sev or roasted peanuts for added texture and flavor.
  14. Serve hot as a snack or breakfast item.
